# Global Malnutrition Study – FAO Data Analysis

## Context
Simulated Data Analyst position within a research team at the 
Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO).
Mission: deliver a comprehensive study on the state of malnutrition 
and food availability worldwide.

## Objectives
- Analyze the proportion of undernourished people globally (2017)
- Estimate the theoretical number of people that could be fed based 
  on worldwide food availability
- Break down domestic food supply: share used for animal feed, 
  food losses, and actual human consumption
- Identify countries most affected by food insecurity
- Highlight countries that benefited most from food aid since 2013

## Data Sources
- FAO datasets: food availability, domestic supply, population, 
  undernourishment indicators
- Geographic and country-level reference data

## Deliverables
- Global overview of malnutrition indicators for 2017
- Country-level breakdown: undernourishment rates, food availability 
  per capita, food aid received
- Key findings and data-driven recommendations

## Tools & Skills
- Python (pandas, numpy)
- Exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- Data cleaning and aggregation
- Statistical analysis and visualization

## Key Learnings
Handling large-scale international datasets, computing derived indicators 
from raw supply data, and communicating findings clearly for 
non-technical stakeholders.
